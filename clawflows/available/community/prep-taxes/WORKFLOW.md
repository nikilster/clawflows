# Prep Your Taxes

Walk through a tax document checklist to help you gather everything you need before filing. Track what you have and what's still missing.

## Steps

1. **Review common documents** — Go through this standard checklist with the user:
   - W-2s (from each employer)
   - 1099s (freelance, interest, dividends, investments)
   - Mortgage interest statement (1098)
   - Student loan interest (1098-E)
   - Health insurance (1095-A/B/C)
   - Charitable donation receipts
   - Property tax records
   - Childcare expenses

2. **Scan email for documents** — Use your **email skill** to search for tax-related emails: W-2 notifications, 1099 delivery alerts, year-end statements, and charity donation confirmations.

3. **Check what's arrived** — Mark each document as received, pending, or not applicable based on what you find.

4. **Identify gaps** — Flag any documents that should have arrived by now but haven't. Most W-2s and 1099s are due by January 31.

5. **Create action items** — For each missing document, suggest a next step (contact employer, check online portal, call the bank, etc.).

## Output Format

```
Tax Prep Checklist — [tax year]

✅ Received:
  W-2 from Acme Corp
  1099-INT from Chase Bank
  Charity receipts ($340 total)

⏳ Pending:
  1099-NEC from freelance client — should arrive by Jan 31
  1098 mortgage interest — check lender's online portal

❌ Still Need:
  Health insurance form (1095) — contact HR

Next Steps:
  1. Log into mortgage portal for 1098
  2. Email freelance client about 1099
  3. Call HR about 1095 form
```

## Notes

- This workflow is a helper, not tax advice. Always recommend consulting a tax professional for complex situations.
- Tax deadlines and document types are based on US federal taxes. Let the user know if they need country-specific guidance.
- Don't store or ask for Social Security numbers, tax IDs, or other sensitive identifiers.
- Run this multiple times as documents trickle in during January and February.
