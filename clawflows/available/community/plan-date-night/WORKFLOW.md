# Plan Date Night

Help the user plan a fun date night each week with fresh ideas that match their budget, schedule, and interests.

## Steps

1. **Check the schedule.** Use your **calendar skill** to see when both partners are free this week. Suggest the best evening for date night, or confirm the user's preferred night.

2. **Consider the basics.** Think about budget (the user can set a range like "under $50" or "free"), weather for outdoor options, and what they've done recently so you don't repeat ideas too soon.

3. **Generate 3 ideas.** Offer three different options across categories:
   - **Go out:** A restaurant, activity, or event nearby
   - **Stay in:** A themed dinner, movie night, game, or project to do together
   - **Wildcard:** Something a little unexpected or new

4. **Help with logistics.** If they pick an option, offer to help with the details — look up the restaurant, find showtimes, or suggest recipes for a stay-in night using your **browser skill**.

5. **Add it to the calendar.** Once they decide, offer to block off the time using your **calendar skill**.

## Output Format

```
💑 Date Night Ideas — This Week

Your schedule looks open Friday and Saturday evening.

Option 1 — Go Out:
  Try that new Thai place on Main Street. Casual,
  good reviews, ~$40 for two.

Option 2 — Stay In:
  Homemade pizza night + a movie you've both been
  wanting to see. Budget: ~$15.

Option 3 — Wildcard:
  Sunset walk at the lake + ice cream on the way back.
  Free (minus the ice cream).

Which sounds good, or want different ideas?
```

## Notes

- Be inclusive of all relationship types. Never assume anything about the couple.
- Respect budget constraints without judgment. Free dates can be the best dates.
- Keep track of what they've done recently so ideas stay fresh.
- If they don't have a partner, this workflow works great for self-dates or friend hangouts too.
- Don't be pushy about going out. Staying in is a perfectly great date night.
