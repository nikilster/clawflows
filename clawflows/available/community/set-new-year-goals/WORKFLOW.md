# Set New Year Goals

A guided year-end reflection and goal-setting session. Look back honestly, look forward intentionally, and walk away with goals that actually stick.

## 1. Reflect on the Past Year

Ask conversationally, one at a time: What are you most proud of? What surprised you? What was harder than expected? What did you let go of? Save reflections to `~/.openclaw/data/goals/{year}-reflection.md`.

## 2. Review Last Year's Goals

If previous goals exist at `~/.openclaw/data/goals/`, check which were completed, partially done, or abandoned. Don't frame abandoned goals as failures — sometimes priorities change.

## 3. Set New Goals

Guide the user to set 3-5 goals. For each, define: what (specific), why (meaningful), first step (this week), and how to track progress. Encourage a mix — wellness, learning, relationships, fun, practical.

## 4. Present

```
🎆 Goals for 2027

LOOKING BACK AT 2026
Proudest: Finished the half marathon
Hardest: The job transition in Q2

LAST YEAR'S GOALS
✅ Run a half marathon — done!
🟡 Read 20 books — got to 14
❌ Learn guitar — bought one, played twice

2027 GOALS
1. 📚 Read 18 books — pick first 3 this week
2. 💪 Strength train 3x/week — sign up by Jan 5
3. 👥 Host dinner party every other month — pick Jan date
4. 💰 Save $5,000 trip fund — auto-transfer $420/month

📅 Quarterly reviews: April 1, July 1, October 1
```

Save to `~/.openclaw/data/goals/{year}-goals.md`. Send via your **messaging skill**.

## Notes

- This is reflective, not a productivity system. Keep the tone warm and unhurried.
- Don't push goals in every category. Three meaningful goals beat seven forced ones.
- If last year's goals were mostly unmet, be gentle. Focus on what they did accomplish.
- Goals should excite the user, not stress them out. Help adjust if something feels heavy.
- Suggest quarterly check-ins rather than daily tracking. Goals need breathing room.
