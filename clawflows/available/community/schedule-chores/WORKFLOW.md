# Schedule Chores

Create a fair weekly chore schedule for everyone in the household, rotating tasks so no one gets stuck with the same job every week.

## Steps

1. **Know the household.** On first run, ask who lives in the household (names or nicknames). Remember this for future weeks. The user can update the list anytime.

2. **Review the chore list.** Use the default chore list or any custom list the user has provided. Common chores include: dishes, vacuuming, bathroom cleaning, laundry, trash/recycling, kitchen wipe-down, and tidying common areas.

3. **Rotate assignments.** Shift chores from last week so everyone gets different tasks. Keep it fair over time — no one should get the least-liked chore two weeks in a row.

4. **Present the schedule.** Show a clean list of who does what this week.

5. **Send it out.** Offer to send each person their assignments using your **messaging skill**.

## Output Format

```
🧹 Chore Schedule — Week of March 23

Alex:    Dishes, take out trash
Jamie:   Vacuum common areas, laundry
Sam:     Clean bathroom, kitchen wipe-down

Rotate next Saturday. Everyone pitches in!
```

## Notes

- Be flexible with household setups. Could be roommates, a family, or just one person wanting structure.
- If someone is away or sick that week, redistribute their chores and note it.
- Never make assumptions about who should do what based on age or gender.
- Keep the tone encouraging, not nagging. Chores are a team effort.
- If someone has a strong preference (loves or hates a specific chore), try to accommodate it.
