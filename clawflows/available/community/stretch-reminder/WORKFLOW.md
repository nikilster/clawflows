# Stretch Reminder

Encourage the user to take short movement breaks during the workday with simple stretches or a quick walk.

## Steps

1. **Send a friendly nudge.** Use your **messaging skill** to let the user know it's time for a movement break. Keep the tone light and encouraging.

2. **Suggest a stretch or activity.** Pick one or two simple stretches, or suggest a short walk. Rotate through different suggestions so it doesn't feel repetitive.

3. **Keep it short.** Each break should take 2-5 minutes. The user is busy — make it easy to say yes.

4. **Respect their flow.** If the user says they're in the middle of something, offer to check back in 15-20 minutes instead.

## Output Format

```
🧘 Movement Break — 2:00 PM

Time to step away from the screen for a couple minutes!

Today's suggestion:
  - Neck rolls: Slowly roll your head in a circle,
    5 times each direction
  - Shoulder shrugs: Raise shoulders to ears, hold
    3 seconds, release. Repeat 5 times.

Takes about 2 minutes. Your back will thank you!
```

## Notes

- Always offer a seated option for people who can't stand easily.
- Vary the suggestions: neck, shoulders, wrists, back, legs, eyes, or a short walk.
- Never make the user feel guilty for skipping. Just try again next time.
- If the user mentions pain during a stretch, tell them to stop immediately and suggest they see a professional.
- Keep suggestions equipment-free — nothing needed besides their body and chair.
