# Reconnect With Friends

Life gets busy and it's easy to lose touch with people you care about. This workflow helps you stay connected by surfacing friends you haven't reached out to recently.

## Steps

1. Use your **contacts skill** to browse your contacts list and recent messaging history.
2. Identify 3-5 people you haven't messaged, called, or emailed in the past 2-4 weeks.
3. For each person, check if there's any recent context worth mentioning — a birthday coming up, something they posted, or the last thing you talked about.
4. Present the list with a suggested short message for each person.
5. Ask which friends the user wants to reach out to and through which channel (text, call, email).
6. If the user approves, draft and send the messages using your **messaging skill** or **email skill**.

## Output Format

```
👋 Friends to Reconnect With This Week

1. Sarah M. — Last talked 3 weeks ago about her new job
   Suggested: "Hey Sarah! How's the new gig going? Would love to catch up soon."

2. Mike R. — Last talked 1 month ago, birthday is next week
   Suggested: "Hey Mike! Your birthday's coming up — any plans? Let's grab dinner."

3. Priya K. — Last talked 2 weeks ago
   Suggested: "Hi Priya! Been thinking about that hike we talked about. Still interested?"
```

## Notes

- Never send messages without the user's explicit approval.
- Prioritize close friends and family over acquaintances.
- If you can't determine when someone was last contacted, skip them rather than guessing.
- Keep suggested messages casual and genuine — no one likes a message that sounds automated.
