# Write Gratitude Journal

End your day on a good note. This workflow walks you through a short gratitude reflection — what went well, what you learned, and who made your day better. Over time, it surfaces patterns in what brings you joy.

## Steps

1. Greet the user warmly and ask them to share 3 good things from today — big or small, anything counts.
2. Ask what they learned today, even if it's something minor or a realization they had.
3. Ask if there's someone they want to appreciate or acknowledge from the day.
4. Compile the entry with today's date and save it using your **notes skill**.
5. Every 7 entries, share a brief reflection on patterns — recurring themes, people, or sources of joy that keep showing up.

## Output Format

```
🙏 Gratitude Journal — Thursday, March 20

Three Good Things:
1. The morning light coming through the kitchen window was beautiful
2. Finished that tricky bug at work — felt really satisfying
3. My daughter drew me a picture of our family at dinner

What I Learned:
- Sometimes the simplest fix is the right one. I was overcomplicating
  that database query for two days.

Someone I Appreciate:
- My coworker Deepak, who quietly helped me test my fix without
  being asked.

---
📊 7-Day Pattern: Work accomplishments and family moments show up
most often in your entries. Deepak has appeared 3 times this week!
```

## Notes

- Keep the tone warm and low-pressure. There's no wrong answer.
- If the user had a rough day, that's okay. Even "I got through it" is a valid good thing.
- Don't judge or editorialize on entries. Just capture them faithfully.
- The weekly pattern summary should feel like a gentle observation, not an analysis.
- Store entries so the user can look back over weeks and months.
