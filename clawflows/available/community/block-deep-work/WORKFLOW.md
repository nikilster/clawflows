# Block Deep Work Time

Scan your calendar for the day and protect your focus time. Find gaps between meetings and block 2-3 hour windows for uninterrupted deep work.

## Steps

1. **Review today's calendar** — Use your **calendar skill** to pull all meetings and events for today (and optionally tomorrow).

2. **Identify fragmented time** — Look for days where meetings are scattered with small gaps in between. Those 30-minute gaps between calls rarely produce good work.

3. **Find deep work windows** — Look for open blocks of 2 hours or more. Morning blocks (before 11am) and afternoon blocks (after 2pm) tend to work best.

4. **Suggest blocks to add** — Recommend specific time slots to block off on the calendar. Name them something clear like "Focus Time" or "Deep Work — No Meetings."

5. **Create the blocks** — With the user's permission, use your **calendar skill** to add the focus time blocks to their calendar.

## Output Format

```
Deep Work Planner — [date]

Today's Schedule:
  9:00  Team standup (30 min)
  10:30 Client call (1 hr)
  1:00  Lunch
  2:00  1-on-1 with manager (30 min)
  4:00  Project review (45 min)

Suggested Focus Blocks:
  ✅ 7:30 - 9:00 — Morning focus (1.5 hrs)
  ✅ 11:30 - 1:00 — Midday deep work (1.5 hrs)
  ✅ 2:30 - 4:00 — Afternoon focus (1.5 hrs)

Want me to add these to your calendar?
```

## Notes

- Always ask before adding events to the user's calendar. Never auto-create without permission.
- Respect existing commitments. Don't suggest moving meetings — just work around them.
- Some people prefer morning focus, others afternoon. Ask the user their preference.
- Even a 90-minute block is valuable. Don't skip it just because it's not a full 2 hours.
- If the day is completely packed with meetings, say so honestly and suggest protecting time tomorrow instead.
